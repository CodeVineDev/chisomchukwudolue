Tesla Stock Risk Analysis
Project Overview
Objective
The objective of this project was to analyse historical stock market data for Tesla, Inc. to understand price volatility, trading activity, and long-term market behaviour. The analysis aimed to identify risk patterns and price movement trends using financial indicators such as moving averages, volatility, and daily returns, and to present these insights through an interactive risk analysis dashboard.
Context
This project was conducted within a financial market analysis context using structured historical stock data. The analysis focuses on understanding how stock price movements, volatility levels, and trading volume evolve over time, helping highlight potential risk periods and overall market behaviour.
Role
Data Analyst (Independent Project)
Duration
March 2026
Tools & Methodologies
The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel, Power Query, and Microsoft Power BI for data cleaning, transformation, and dashboard development.
Methodologies applied included:
Time-series analysis
Descriptive statistics
Risk indicator analysis
KPI development
Interactive dashboard visualisation
These methods helped transform historical stock data into meaningful insights about volatility and price behaviour.
Approach & Process
Data Collection
The dataset used for this project consisted of historical stock data for Tesla from 2010 to 2025.
Key variables included:
Date
Open price
High price
Low price
Close price
Trading volume
Daily return
Price range
Price change percentage
Moving averages (MA7, MA30, MA90)
7-day volatility
Year, Month, Day of week, and Quarter
These variables enabled both time-series analysis and risk evaluation.
Data Cleaning & Preparation
Data preparation was conducted using Power Query to ensure the dataset was accurate and analysis-ready.
The dataset was reviewed to confirm:
Correct data types for financial metrics
Consistency in date formatting
Absence of duplicate records
During the preparation stage:
Duplicate checks were performed across key financial variables and the date column.
No duplicate records were identified.
Blank values in the moving average columns (MA7, MA30, MA90) were retained because they represent periods where insufficient prior data existed to compute the moving averages.
This ensured that financial indicators remained statistically valid.
Exploratory Data Analysis
Initial exploration was performed to understand overall stock behaviour and price movement patterns.
Summary analysis focused on:
Distribution of daily returns
Long-term price movement trends
Trading volume patterns across years
Volatility fluctuations over time
This exploratory stage helped identify the most relevant indicators for analysing stock risk and volatility.
Analysis Techniques
Risk Analysis Approach
The risk analysis focused on evaluating stock behaviour using several financial indicators commonly used in market analysis.
These indicators included:
Moving averages to identify long-term price trends
Volatility measures to evaluate market instability
Daily return patterns to understand price fluctuations
Price range analysis to measure daily market movement
This approach allowed the analysis to capture both trend behaviour and risk signals within the stock data.
Metrics & KPIs
Key performance indicators were calculated to provide a high-level overview of Tesla’s stock performance:
Total trading volume
Average closing price
Average daily return
Average price range
Average volatility (7-day)
These KPIs summarise overall market activity and risk behaviour.
Visualisations & Insights
An interactive Power BI dashboard was developed to present the analysis clearly and intuitively.
The dashboard includes visualisations highlighting:
Moving average trends (MA7, MA30, MA90)
Yearly volatility trends
Volatility patterns across quarters
Distribution of daily returns
Average price range trends
These visualisations allow users to explore stock performance across different time periods and identify periods of higher market risk.
Key Findings
The analysis revealed several important patterns:
Stock volatility fluctuates significantly across different years, indicating varying levels of market risk.
Moving average trends highlight long-term price growth periods and short-term fluctuations.
Certain quarters demonstrate higher volatility levels compared to others, suggesting seasonal market behaviour.
Price range analysis shows periods where daily market movement increases, signalling higher uncertainty in trading conditions.
